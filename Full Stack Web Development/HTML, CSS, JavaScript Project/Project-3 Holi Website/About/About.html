Holi Famous Festival Of India

Holi is an important festival of Indian and Nepalese people celebrated in spring. This festival is celebrated on the full moon day of Phalgun month according to the Hindu calendar.Holika is burnt on the first day, which is also known as Holika Dahan. On the second day, which is mainly known as Dhulendi and Dhurddi, Dhurkhel or Dhulivandan, people throw colors, abir-gulal etc. on each other, Holi songs are sung by playing drums and people are smeared with colors by going from house to house. It is believed that on the day of Holi people forget the old bitterness and hug and become friends again. The phase of coloring each other and singing goes on till noon. After this, after taking bath and resting, wearing new clothes, people visit each other's house in the evening, hug and feed sweets.This popular festival of Raga-Rang is also the messenger of spring. [4] Raga means music and color are its main parts, but the nature that takes them to the heights is also at its peak with colorful youth at this time. Due to being celebrated in the month of Falgun, it is also called Falguni. The festival of Holi starts from Vasant Panchami itself. Gulal is blown for the first time on the same day. From this day the song of Phag and Dhamar starts. Mustard blossoms in the fields. There is an attractive shade of flowers in the gardens.Trees-plants, animals-birds and humans all become full of joy. The ears of wheat start flaunting in the fields. Forgetting all inhibitions and conventions, children and old people get immersed in dance-music and colors with the tune of dholak-jhanj-manjirs. There is a splash of colors all around.[5] The main dish of Holi is Gujiya which is made of mawa (khoya) and refined flour and is filled with dry fruits.There is also a custom of eating and feeding Kanji on this day. On the evening of Holi, wearing new clothes, people go to each other's house to meet Holi, where they are welcomed with Gujiya, Namkeen and Thandai. On the day of Holi, eating mango manjari and sandalwood together has great significance.
